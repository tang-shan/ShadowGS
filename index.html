Shadow-Aware 3D Gaussian Splatting via Lambertian-Guided Neural Masking

Abstract

Recently, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has demonstrated impressive performance in novel view synthesis due to its efficient explicit scene representation and real-time rendering. However, its reconstruction quality significantly degrades in the presence of accidental shadows within input images. We observe that 3DGS tends to misinterpret shadow regions as view-dependent reflectance variations, such as specular highlights or Fresnel effects, leading to persistent artifacts in synthesized views. To address this issue, we propose a shadow-aware optimization framework that explicitly identifies and suppresses accidental shadows during training. Specifically, we first derive candidate shadow masks from shadow-free renderings to provide coarse shadow localization. A residual-based neural classifier is then employed to refine these masks by modeling photometric inconsistencies. Furthermore, a Lambertian reflectance constraint is imposed within the detected shadow regions to regularize view-dependent components and prevent shadows from being misinterpreted as specular effects. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method effectively eliminates shadow-induced artifacts while preserving geometric consistency and rendering fidelity. Compared with vanilla 3DGS, our approach achieves consistently improved reconstruction quality across multiple datasets while maintaining the high optimization efficiency characteristic of 3DGS.
